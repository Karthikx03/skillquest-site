About SkillQuest

The mission, the approach, and the commitment to free, practical education for everyone.

Education is a right, not a privilege

SkillQuest started with a simple observation: schools teach academic subjects well, but they rarely teach the practical skills people need most. How to manage money. How to stay safe online. How to communicate professionally. How to think entrepreneurially.

I built SkillQuest to fill that gap — and because I believe tools like this should be accessible to everyone, it is and will always be free.

The gamification is not cosmetic. Research shows that clear goals, immediate feedback, and a sense of progress meaningfully improve learning retention. Points and leaderboards exist because they work, not because they look fun in a pitch deck.

The Story

Why I built this

SkillQuest is a project I built while studying at a university in Taiwan. I kept noticing the same problem: students around me had strong academic records but struggled with basic financial decisions, couldn't identify a phishing email, and had no idea how to write a professional message to a potential employer.

So I built SkillQuest to teach those things — in short, digestible lessons with quiz-based reinforcement. I kept it free because charging for something this fundamental felt wrong.
